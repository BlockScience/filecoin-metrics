Filecoin Metrics

an overview of system health and performance

About

Data driven governance requires data.

Deriving insights from data requires maps and models.

This site contains a series of notebooks that pull live data from the Filecoin network, run it through models, and then visualize key metrics across a set of dashboards.

This can provide insights to guide FIP discussions and stakeholder decisions to improve the Filecoin network.

Sector State Metrics Dashboard

Notebook

The Sector State Metrics Dashboard shows missing Proofs of Space Time (PoST) over time. This is important because missing PoST implies unreliable storage services. By monitoring the % of PoST faults we can verify that:

  • the protocol is functioning as intended and terminating faulty sectors
  • the % of sector faults is staying within an acceptable range

Sector Life Metrics Dashboard

Notebook

This dashboard can show us the lifetime of sectors.

This is important to:

  • anticipate when sectors will expire
  • compare the ratio of sector expirations vs activations
  • understand the average lifetime of sectors

When sectors expire miners can either reinvest the FIL and equipment into a new sector, or sell them on the market.

  • If there are more sector activations than expirations that puts positive price pressure on FIL and mining equipment.
  • If there are more expirations than activations that might show that miners find storage provision to be unprofitable and are waiting for market conditions to change or dumping capital (FIL and equipment) on the market.

Gas EDA V1

Notebook

In this notebook we perform Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on FIL's gas fee mechanism. The goal is to observe the gas fee as a signal and attempt to understand what may be driving it. We perform Fourier Transformations on the minute aggregated derived_gas_outputs data.

Gas EDA V2

Notebook

Builds on Gas EDA V1 and performs Fourier Transformations on message_gas_economy data. Additionally, chain_rewards, chain_powers, and chain_economics event level data is analyzed and Granger Causality is performed on the signals.
